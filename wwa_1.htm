Bruce Jones, Managing Director

Bruce graduated in 1978 with a degree in Mechanical Engineering and quickly started a career in Construction. Of this, over 26 years has been involved in Mechanical Systems with over 17 years experience specifically in the field of Façade Access.

This began in earnest at the construction of Heathrow Terminal 4 project with Taylor Woodrow Construction, managing the installation of all the mechanical handling equipment. Then moving on to the Gatwick North Terminal project with French Kier.

Following an approach from Kobi Group PLC / Kobi Cradles Ltd in 1988, Bruce joined as Group Director responsible for the cradle and contracting division. The period with Kobi saw the company grow to be the market leaders in the supply and installation of façade access equipment in the UK.

In 1991 Bruce joined forces with Grant Judge Partnership to expand and diversify the consultancy practice, developing new areas of expertise that were built on the sound experience contained within the practice. Initially providing a professional service in the field of building facade maintenance systems, and developing the fuller field of mechanical handling.

This progressed on to establish the façade access division of Lerch Bates & Associates in the UK following an approach from their U.S. operation to provide consultancy services in the field of building maintenance access systems.

Finally in 1998, Bruce established REEF to provide an independent specialist consulting practice in the field of Façade Access.
 
Chris Whyte, Director

Chris has over 15 years experience in the field of façade access. This began at Kobi Cradles Ltd in 1990 who were the market leaders in the supply and installation of facade access equipment in the UK.

Chris was responsible for the day to day running of individual contracts including design work, contract management and close liaison with clients and architects. This progressed to becoming Contracts Manager, responsible for the overall management and running of the Contracts Department. Over this period additional international experience was obtained through the development of relationships with European equipment manufacturers.

In 1995, Chris joined the European Offices of Lerch, Bates and Associates FAC Ltd to assist in the expansion of the practice in the field of building access maintenance systems.

Finally in 1998, Chris joined REEF to provide independent specialist consulting advice in the field of Façade Access.
